# SIFF 2026 Conflict Guide

An unofficial planner for the Seattle International Film Festival, May 7–17, 2026.

[**Open the planner →**](https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/REPO-NAME/) <!-- replace with your URL -->

## What it does

The official SIFF program lists 200+ screenings across 11 days and 6 venues. Figuring out which films overlap, which to skip, and which are worth your evening can be exhausting from the printed grid. This planner shows you the whole festival on one scrollable page, designed around the question that actually matters: **what am I going to see, and what do I have to skip to see it?**

## How to use it

**Browse the schedule.** Each day is a tab at the top. Films that overlap are grouped together in pink-bordered "Conflict · pick one" slots so you can see at a glance where you'll have to make a choice.

**Tap a film card to expand it.** You'll see the synopsis, director, country, runtime, trailer link, and a button to buy tickets directly on siff.net.

**Star the films you want to see.** The ★ in the top-right of any card adds it to your picks. Open the **★ My Picks** tab (first tab) to see everything you've saved, grouped by day, with warnings if you've starred two films that overlap.

**Mark films you're not interested in.** The ⊘ in the top-left dims all showings of a film so you remember next time you scroll past. Toggle the "Hide Not Interested" filter to make them disappear entirely.

**See what the programmers recommend.** Films picked by SIFF's 20 festival programmers show a "★ N picks" badge. Expand the card to read each programmer's personal blurb. There's a filter to show only recommended films.

**Filter by venue.** Pills at the top let you narrow to just Uptown, Downtown, Film Center, PacSci, or Paramount.

## Your picks save automatically

Your stars and "not interested" marks save in your browser, so they'll be there next time you open the page. They stay on the device you set them on though — if you star a film on your laptop, you won't see it starred on your phone.

## A few things to know

- **This is unofficial.** It's a fan project, not affiliated with SIFF. For ticket purchases, last-minute changes, or anything official, the source of truth is always [siff.net](https://www.siff.net/festival). Every film card has a "Tickets & info" link that takes you straight to its official SIFF page.
- **The schedule is a snapshot** taken in late April 2026. Films occasionally get added, rescheduled, or sell out as the festival approaches.
- **No app to install, no account to make.** It's just a web page. Open it, use it, close it.

## Hosting your own copy

The whole thing is a single HTML file. To put it online for friends:

- **GitHub Pages**: rename to `index.html`, push to a repo, enable Pages in Settings.
- **Netlify Drop**: drag the file onto [app.netlify.com/drop](https://app.netlify.com/drop). Live URL in under a minute.
- **Just send the file**: works fine over AirDrop, email, or a shared cloud folder. Anyone can open it directly in their browser.
